Essential Idea
Stories with underlying cautionary messages seek to warn society against the dangerous traits of human nature.

Stories are fantastic mediums of expression. They are intrinsically engaging, tapping into our empathy to create emotional connections. Authors take advantage of these mediums, externalizing their own agenda upon their stories. Specifically, I will be focusing on how stories from different genres, forms, and periods seek to guide humanity towards a better future by exemplifying the consequences that the dark side of human nature brings, ultimately making their impact on humanity.

Andrew Neiman is a jazz drummer studying at a music academy where he meets Terence Fletcher‚Äîthe notorious maestro of the academy's jazz band. Leonard Mead lives in the year 2053 in a large yet desolate city where technology has advanced. Traversing the city during his nightly walks brings Leonard a feeling of loneliness as the whole city is at home captivated by their television. Leonard is eventually stopped by a robotic police car, questioning his motives for walking. The police car dismisses Leonard‚Äôs profession of being a writer as not legitimate and his act of walking being unorthodox, leading to Leonard‚Äôs banishment to a research facility on regressive tendencies.

  • Conformity -As society has begun to rely upon technology, individuality is stripped away.   • Loss of Humanity -Humans are inherently social and creative, yet in this society where technology dominates every aspect of one's life, these core elements of what makes humans human are systematically diminished. Being confined to one's home and forbade from walking creates isolation and a lack of social interaction. Technology has made our lives easier, for better or worse, but when does its convenience affect our humanity?

The Significance of Civilization

A group of boys is stranded on an island without adults due to an ongoing war. Immediately, two boys, Ralph and Jack, emerge as candidates for leadership. Ralph advocates for civilization and order, while Jack advocates for chaos and fun. Ralph is initially the leader until fear brings out the savagery within the boys, destroying the civilization Ralph attempts to create and causing them to favor Jack more. As logic and morality fade, the island descends into full savagery, ending with the death of two boys. The novel ends with the boys being rescued by a naval officer, returning to the war-ridden world.

  • A Damper -Civilization acts as a damper for the inner evil that resides in all humans. Under Ralph's rule, progress towards leaving the island is made, temporarily hindering the savagery inside the boys. However, the makeshift civilization created by Ralph was weak, with no actual enforcement or legitimacy, fear naturally wrecks it. However, on a broader scale, civilization can never truly prevent the natural evil of humans, highlighted by the underlying problem in the novel: war.
  • Natural Evil -Jack symbolizes the evil that resides in all humans. His constant attempts at undermining Ralph's civilization indicate how dificult it is to suppress this evil. As Jack uses fear to rule the boys, they dive into his savage ways and give in to their desires, including murder.

Civilization, order, and rules‚Äîwe have always taken these as inadvertently absolute, yet how significant are they really?

Repercussions of Impulsivity

Shakespeare‚Äôs Romeo and Juliet follows the tragic tale of two lovers, Romeo and Juliet, born into feuding families. Despite the conflict with society, Romeo and Juliet stay determined to be together, and within the span of five days after first meeting, they get married and die tragically. Many factors led to this tragedy, but they all have one thing in common: they stem from impulse. By letting emotion dictate their actions rather than logic, a flawed plan and multiple deaths are derived. However, Romeo and Juliet‚Äôs deaths are the turning point for their families, realizing the harm their hatred brings, allowing their relationship to heal.

  • Impulsivity -The tragedy of the play is driven through impulse. The duration of which the events occur, over five days, is the biggest indicator. Being a short period of time in which tragedy occurs over highlights how the actions made were throughout the play were not well-planned and done with a lack of foresight.
  • Selfishness -The impulsivity demonstrated throughout the play is reflective of the selfishness of each character. Romeo and Juliet's decision to get married despite the obvious disapproval from society and their parents, dragging along others into the tragedy just to see their goal through, highlight this. Even when their relationship causes the death of another, they continue out of self-interest, disregarding the harm they caused to others.

Humans make decisions with a mix of emotion and logic, but what happens when we let our feelings override reason?

  • The Consequences -As Mitchell already states, you don't realize what you have until it's gone, humans take nature and all its beauty for granted, but once its all been exploited and becomes history, humans will realize how important nature is. This goes far beyond nature's aesthetic value‚Äîthe environment is crucial for sustaining life and the fact that humans realize this yet continue to act in neglectful manners exemplefies the power greed has over humans.
